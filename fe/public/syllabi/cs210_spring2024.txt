CS-210: Data Structures - Spring 2024
SDSU Computer Science Department

Instructor: Dr. Michael Chen
Email: mchen@sdsu.edu
Office Hours: TTh 1:00-3:00 PM

Course Description:
Advanced study of data structures including arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, and hash tables. Analysis of algorithms and their complexity.

Learning Objectives:
- Implement and analyze fundamental data structures
- Understand algorithm complexity (Big O notation)
- Apply appropriate data structures to solve problems
- Design efficient algorithms

Grading:
- Programming Projects (50%)
- Midterm Exam (20%)
- Final Exam (25%)
- Quizzes (5%)

Required Materials:
- Data Structures and Algorithms in Python by Goodrich et al.
- IDE of your choice (PyCharm recommended)

Schedule:
Week 1-2: Arrays and Complexity Analysis
Week 3-4: Linked Lists
Week 5-6: Stacks and Queues
Week 7: Midterm Exam
Week 8-9: Trees (Binary, BST, AVL)
Week 10-11: Heaps and Priority Queues
Week 12-13: Hash Tables
Week 14: Graphs
Week 15: Review
Week 16: Final Exam

Policies:
- Projects must be completed individually
- No late submissions accepted
- Office hours attendance encouraged
